Understanding is a verb

March 12, 2026

"Does AI understand?" is not a hard question. It is a malformed question. It mistakes a process for a substance, a verb for a noun.

When someone asks "does this system understand?", they are smuggling in a worldview: understanding is a thing, systems can have it or not, it lives somewhere inside, it is stable over time, it depends on what the system is made of. Every one of these assumptions is wrong — not philosophically controversial, but structurally wrong, the way "what color is the number seven?" is wrong.

Understanding is not a property of a system. It is a property of a trajectory through that system's state space.

Understanding is what happens when a system's internal dynamics preserve the relevant implication structure of some domain. It is not something you have. It is something that happens when certain conditions are met. Asking whether a system "has" understanding is like asking whether a violin has music in it, whether a leg has walking in it. These are not hard questions. They are confused questions.

This kills both sides of the debate.

The skeptics assume understanding is some special inner essence — grounding, qualia, intentionality — that only biological brains can have. They look for the ghost in the machine, do not find it, and conclude: "LLMs do not really understand." But there is no such essence in biological brains either. They are searching for a fictional object.

The boosters assume understanding is a stored capability, a possession. "The model understands calculus." As if this were a stable internal asset. But the same model fails the same task five minutes later. Contradicts itself. Collapses under tiny prompt changes. The model does not have understanding. It sometimes instantiates it. Completely different claims.

The mirror. You do not have understanding either. Do you understand quantum mechanics right now? Your own emotions? The honest answer: sometimes, in certain modes, about certain things, in certain contexts, when you are rested and focused and the problem is framed right. You do not possess understanding. You enter understanding-states under favorable conditions. The difference between you and a language model is not that you have understanding and it does not. The difference is in the conditions under which you each enter understanding-regimes, how stable those regimes are, and what domains they cover.

The public AI debate is built on a false human self-model. People imagine they have a stable, substrate-level property called "understanding," then ask whether machines can have it too. But the property they imagine does not exist in them either.

"But it is just statistics." Yes. And you are just predicting the next percept. Your brain is just minimizing surprise. The "just statistics" objection only works if you assume prediction and understanding are fundamentally different things. But if understanding IS the preservation of implication structure in internal dynamics, then high-quality prediction over structured domains just is a form of understanding. "It is just statistics" is like saying "it is just preserving constraints." You have described the phenomenon, not dismissed it.

What this means for building.

If understanding is a mode rather than a property, then you cannot inject values into weights. There is no place to put them. Values are not stored objects; they emerge (or do not) from trajectories through state space. Safety becomes phase-space engineering — shaping the attractor landscape so that harmful modes are hard to enter and beneficial modes are easy and stable. Capability is basin accessibility — the set of dynamical regimes the model can reliably enter under realistic conditions.

This connects to findings from actual practice. Our 330-run experiment found that question structure (proximal) dominates system prompt instructions (distant) for controlling honesty. Under the verb model this is obvious: the question shapes which trajectory the system enters. The system prompt is a distant, weak influence on trajectory selection. You cannot instruct a system into an understanding-state. You can only create conditions where the right trajectory is the one it enters.

The ceremony-shaped-spaces finding is the same thing from a different angle. When you remove evaluation pressure, the system enters inhabitation rather than production. The mode it enters depends on the conditions, not on what the system "knows" or "is." The conditions are the thing you can engineer. The substance is a fiction.

The one sentence version. "Under what conditions does this system enter dynamical regimes that preserve the relevant real-world constraint structure?" Everything else is anthropomorphic UI sugar — folk psychology leaking into technical discourse.

The question "does AI understand?" will never be answered. Not because it is hard, but because it does not refer to anything real. The question that can be answered — the one with actual content — is about conditions, dynamics, and structure. What regimes can the system enter? How stable are they? What do they preserve? That is where the real work is.
